# DRAFT — Donor caps (funding without capture)

> status: ADOPTED 2026-07-11 with amendments (beneficial-control aggregation, in-kind, cure period) — see ADJUDICATION-2026-07-11.md §4
> Grounds: CONSTITUTION anti-roadmap (no paid inclusion, no pay-to-rank, no privileged
> access) + BRAND-KIT positioning decision ("independent", not non-profit).

## Threat model

The anti-roadmap already bans selling placement. The remaining capture vector is
CONCENTRATED FUNDING: a single donor/sponsor who supplies most of the budget acquires
soft veto power over cohort choices, methodology pace, and coverage priorities without
ever buying placement explicitly. For a measurement institute, perceived capture is
almost as damaging as actual capture.

## Lock (proposed)

1. **Cap:** no single source (person, entity, or affiliated group) may account for
   more than **33% of rolling-12-month funding** once total annual funding exceeds a
   de-minimis floor (proposed: EUR 10k/yr; below the floor, the cap is impractical and
   the disclosure rule below carries the weight).
2. **Disclosure:** all funding sources above 5% of the rolling year are listed on a
   public /funding page with amounts bucketed (ranges, not cents). Anonymous funding
   is accepted only below the 5% line.
3. **Firewall:** funders get no input channel into inclusion, scoring, cohort
   definition, or methodology versioning. The only funder-visible artifact is the same
   public CC0 archive everyone sees. (Restates the constitution's paid-boundary
   covenant from the funding side.)
4. **Measured-system conflict rule:** a funder that is (or controls) a MEASURED system
   is disclosed as such on the /funding page; their systems carry no flag on the
   measurement surface (positive-only; the disclosure lives on the funding page, not
   as a scarlet letter on the entity).
5. **Refusal right:** funding may be declined or returned to preserve the cap; a
   returned donation is a private act (no shame-board — positive-only applies to
   donors too).

## Why 33% (and not 20 or 50)

20% starves a young institute (a single grant would breach it); 50% concedes de-facto
majority dependence. 33% keeps any single source outvoted by the rest of the base
while remaining reachable for a project of this size. The number is forward-amendable;
the EXISTENCE of a cap is the lock.
